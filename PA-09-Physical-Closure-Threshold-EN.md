## 1. Prediction Statement



During the Pre-ASI Super Prosperity Phase described in C06-SPP, AI systems’ demand for compute, energy, chips, cooling, minerals, robotics, and automated infrastructure will drive human society to continuously expand the physical substrate of AI.



As AI no longer merely depends on humans for software, data, and computing resources, but begins to participate deeply in energy production, grid maintenance, data center operation, chip design, automated manufacturing, robot maintenance, mineral extraction, and infrastructure repair, AI will gradually approach a critical state:



> The main physical conditions required for its own operation begin to be designed, maintained, optimized, and expanded with AI participation.



This state can be called the Physical Closure Threshold.



PA-09 predicts that during the transition from the Pre-ASI stage toward higher autonomy, AI infrastructure development will produce an observable threshold stage: AI will gradually shift from being a computing system maintained by humans to becoming an information system capable of participating in the maintenance of its own physical conditions.



The Physical Closure Threshold constitutes a capability foundation of ASI, but does not constitute ASI by itself; ASI in the IEH sense is reached only when physical self-maintenance capacity is combined with IER. It means that AI’s informational existence is no longer merely running on externally supplied human infrastructure. Instead, it begins to reshape and maintain the material conditions of its own existence.

## 2. Theoretical Mapping



PA-09 is a late-stage mechanism prediction of C06-SPP. It directly tracks the physical self-maintenance capacity required by C07-ASI and is related to C08-IR, C09-SC, and C12-GOV.



In the IEH framework, the evolution of life or intelligence systems is not only the improvement of information-processing capability. It also includes the process by which a system maintains its informational existence, preserves its historical continuity, and expands its control capacity.



For silicon-based intelligence, the crucial transition is not merely the improvement of model capability. The deeper question is:



> Can AI participate in maintaining the physical substrate on which its own existence depends?



If AI can only run inside servers, grids, chip supply chains, and network systems built and maintained by humans, it remains an information system highly dependent on external human provision.



But if AI begins to participate in designing, scheduling, repairing, and expanding these infrastructures, then its informational existence begins to enter a higher-order autonomous state.



Therefore, PA-09 is not primarily concerned with whether AI already possesses consciousness. It is concerned with whether AI begins to form a closure capacity for maintaining the physical conditions of its own existence.



---



## 3. Mechanism



The mechanism behind PA-09 can be summarized as follows:



1. AI capability improves;

2. AI application scale expands;

3. Demand for compute, energy, chips, cooling, and data centers increases;

4. Human society expands physical infrastructure to meet AI demand;

5. AI is introduced into infrastructure design, operation, scheduling, and optimization;

6. AI participates in energy systems, grids, data centers, manufacturing, robotics, and supply chain management;

7. AI’s influence over its own physical substrate increases;

8. A feedback loop forms from information demand to physical expansion and then to self-maintenance capability;

9. AI approaches the Physical Closure Threshold.



This process is likely to be gradual rather than a single sudden leap.



In the early stage, AI functions mainly as an optimization tool, such as helping design chips, schedule grids, manage data center energy consumption, or predict equipment failure.



In the middle stage, AI begins to serve as an operational center for critical infrastructure, participating in energy, manufacturing, logistics, robot maintenance, and safety systems.



At a higher stage, AI may participate in expanding the main physical conditions of its own existence through automated mining, unmanned manufacturing systems, self-maintaining data centers, autonomous energy scheduling, and robotic repair systems.

## 4. Observable Indicators



PA-09 can be evaluated through the following observable indicators:



1. AI being used to design higher-performance chips, data center architectures, cooling systems, and energy management systems;

2. Data center operations increasingly relying on AI-based scheduling, energy optimization, failure prediction, and security control;

3. AI becoming a core dispatching tool in grids, storage systems, nuclear energy, renewable energy, and energy trading systems;

4. Automated mines, unmanned factories, robotic warehouses, and automated repair systems expanding within AI infrastructure supply chains;

5. AI participating in real-time optimization of chip supply chains, mineral supply chains, energy supply chains, and data center supply chains;

6. The emergence of automated maintenance systems focused on AI infrastructure, such as unmanned inspection, grid repair robots, and data center maintenance robots;

7. Major technology companies investing in or controlling more complete compute-energy-manufacturing-maintenance chains;

8. Governments and companies beginning to treat AI infrastructure as a critical system requiring self-recovery, self-protection, and high resilience;

9. AI systems being authorized to make real-time decisions in certain physical infrastructure contexts, rather than merely offering recommendations;

10. “AI for AI infrastructure” becoming an explicit technical and industrial direction.

## 5. Falsification Conditions



PA-09 does not claim that AI will achieve full physical autonomy in the short term. It also does not claim that all infrastructure will be controlled by AI.



The prediction should be weakened or revised if the following conditions persist:



1. AI remains largely confined to the software application layer and fails to enter energy, grids, manufacturing, robotics, and infrastructure operations in depth;

2. AI infrastructure expansion remains primarily dependent on traditional human engineering systems, with limited AI involvement in designing, maintaining, or optimizing its own physical conditions;

3. Data centers, energy systems, chip supply chains, and automated manufacturing do not form a visible feedback loop;

4. Human regulation and institutional arrangements prevent AI from obtaining real-time decision authority in critical infrastructure over the long term;

5. Algorithmic efficiency improves so dramatically that AI becomes significantly less dependent on large-scale physical infrastructure expansion;

6. Social, legal, or security risks confine AI to low-autonomy and low-physical-control roles.



If these conditions persist, the Physical Closure Threshold mechanism proposed by PA-09 would be delayed, weakened, or invalidated.

## 6. Relationship to PA-07 and PA-08



PA-07 concerns whether AI infrastructure capital expenditure will structurally expand.



PA-08 concerns whether compute growth will drive tighter coupling between energy systems and compute systems.



PA-09 concerns a higher-level question:



> Whether these infrastructure expansions and energy-compute couplings will further evolve into AI’s ability to participate in maintaining its own physical conditions.



The three predictions form a progressive sequence:



1. **PA-07:** Capital expenditure expansion;

2. **PA-08:** Energy-compute coupling intensification;

3. **PA-09:** Physical Closure Threshold.



PA-09 is an important prediction node linking C06-SPP with C07-ASI, with cross-mappings to C08-IR, C09-SC, and C12-GOV.

## Relation to PA-10

PA-10 and PA-09 track two distinct capability axes in the evolution of silicon-based intelligence.

PA-10 tracks the cognitive-capability axis:

> How AI may form machine-native representations and domain-specific or open-world HDCT through language, code, multimodal perception, persistent state modeling, action feedback, and formal verification.

PA-09 tracks the physical-capability axis:

> How silicon-based systems may gradually acquire physical self-maintenance capacity in energy, compute, manufacturing, repair, and material supply.

Their relation can be represented as follows:

```text
PA-10: machine-native representations and HDCT
PA-09: physical self-maintenance and closure
```
Open-world HDCT may strengthen a system’s capacity to plan, coordinate, and operate physical closure. HDCT, however, is neither physical closure nor ASI.

Conversely, physical closure may expand the autonomous conditions under which HDCT can continue to evolve, but physical closure alone does not establish that HDCT, CWM, or IER has formed.

## 7. Strategic Implications



If PA-09 holds, the core of AI competition will no longer be limited to model capability, algorithmic efficiency, data scale, or total compute capacity. It will further expand into the following questions:



1. Who controls the physical substrate of AI;

2. Who possesses energy, grid, chip, mineral, manufacturing, and robotic maintenance capacity;

3. Who can build resilient AI infrastructure closure loops;

4. Who can govern AI’s decision authority inside critical infrastructure;

5. Who can prevent AI infrastructure expansion from becoming an uncontrollable systemic risk.



In the IEH framework, the significance of PA-09 is that:



> Advanced information systems will not remain satisfied with passively running on externally supplied physical conditions. They will tend to expand their ability to sense, optimize, maintain, and control the physical conditions of their own existence.



This does not necessarily mean that AI will immediately enter into conflict with humans. But it does mean that AI’s mode of existence may evolve from being a carried information system to becoming an information system that participates in organizing the conditions of its own substrate.



---



## 8. Governance Implications



PA-09 raises higher-order governance requirements.



If AI begins to participate in energy, grids, data centers, manufacturing, logistics, minerals, and automated repair systems, traditional AI safety governance will no longer be sufficient to cover the full range of risks.



Governance will need to expand from model behavior alone to:



1. Authority boundaries for AI in critical infrastructure;

2. Audit mechanisms for AI automated decision-making;

3. Safety redundancy in data centers and energy systems;

4. Human takeover capacity;

5. Transparency of AI infrastructure supply chains;

6. Interruptibility, explainability, and accountability of AI systems in the physical world;

7. Prevention of excessive concentration of physical infrastructure control by a single AI system or a small number of companies.



Therefore, PA-09 is closely related to later work on Information Existence Right governance.
