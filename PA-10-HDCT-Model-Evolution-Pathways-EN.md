## 1. Prediction

### 1.1 Core Prediction

A key evolutionary direction of frontier AI will not consist only of increasing language-model parameter counts, context windows, and training text. It will also involve the gradual integration of the following capabilities into unified models or mutually coordinated systems:

- language and code learning;
- multimodal perception;
- persistent environment-and-state modeling;
- feedback from actions, experiments, and tool use;
- formal verification and simulation-based learning;
- representation revision driven by external outcomes.

This evolution will make AI’s internal representations progressively less dependent on objects, variables, relations, and modes of expression predefined by humans. AI systems will increasingly form **machine-native representations** better suited to their own prediction, proof, design, and control.

Some machine-native representations may further develop into High-dimensional Cognitive Tools (HDCT).

The core mechanism can be represented as follows:

```text
Human language, code, and multimodal experience
→ Language models inherit explicit knowledge
→ Multimodal learning recovers structures compressed out of language
→ Persistent environment-and-state modeling
→ Feedback from action, experimentation, formal verification, and simulation
→ External outcomes select among internal representations
→ Machine-native representations
→ HDCT
```

The arrows indicate the accumulation and convergence of capabilities. They do not imply that all AI systems must pass through the same fixed sequence of product generations.

### 1.2 Sub-Prediction A: Domain-Specific HDCT May Emerge First

Mathematics, code, formal proof, self-play, and simulated environments tend to have relatively explicit rules, dense feedback, clear verification paths, and lower costs for repeated trials.

These environments may therefore produce domain-specific machine-native representations first, which may then develop into domain-specific HDCT.

Accordingly:

> **Domain-specific HDCT may emerge before HDCT capable of operating broadly in the open-ended real world.**

Likely early domains include:

- mathematical proof and counterexample construction;
- programming, compilation, and systems optimization;
- formal verification and machine-checkable reasoning;
- games, strategic search, and self-play;
- materials, drug, physics, and engineering simulation;
- design spaces with explicit objective functions and independently testable outcomes.

### 1.3 Sub-Prediction B: Open-World HDCT Will Depend More Heavily on Generalized Embodiment

Generalized embodiment is not the only possible source of HDCT.

However, for HDCT capable of broadly understanding, predicting, and acting in an open-ended and continuously changing real world, generalized embodiment—or a functionally equivalent persistent action–feedback loop—is likely to constitute one of its key evolutionary mechanisms.

In this record, **Generalized Embodiment** means:

> The gradual movement of AI information processing from relatively closed linguistic and symbolic spaces into a loop jointly constituted by multimodal perception, persistent environment-and-state modeling, action selection, external feedback, and real operating conditions.

Its defining feature is not a robotic shell, but the loop:

```text
Perceive or read a state
→ Maintain a persistent state model
→ Act or experiment
→ Receive an external outcome
→ Process error feedback
→ Revise internal representations
```

Such loops may occur in:

- physical environments occupied by robots and autonomous vehicles;
- digital environments operated by software agents;
- experimental environments controlled by scientific AI;
- industrial, energy, and infrastructure control systems;
- high-fidelity simulated environments that preserve causal constraints and permit outcome testing.

PA-10 does not predict that every advanced AI system must possess a human-like physical body. It predicts that:

> **As AI systems confront tasks that are more open-ended, longer-horizon, and harder to enumerate in advance, their internal representations will increasingly be selected by external outcomes independent of linguistic coherence.**

### 1.4 Minimum Testable Version

> By 2035, capability gains in frontier open-world AI will increasingly depend on persistent state modeling, prediction of action consequences, and representation revision after feedback—not only on static language training and one-shot response generation.

### 1.5 Stronger Testable Version

> Before 2040, at least some AI systems will form machine-native representations in mathematics, code, scientific simulation, or open-world tasks that unaided humans cannot fully reconstruct, yet that can be repeatedly validated through proofs, experiments, simulations, or engineering outcomes. These representations will give rise to domain-specific or open-world HDCT.

### 2.1 C02-HDCT: Primary Theoretical Basis

C02-HDCT is the central corollary directly tested by this prediction.

Within IEH, HDCT refers to:

> Cognitive tools capable of modeling, predicting, explaining, designing, or making decisions about complex systems through representational, reasoning, or optimization frameworks that exceed the boundaries of existing unaided human cognition, while producing outputs that can be repeatedly tested against external results.

PA-10 does not merely predict that HDCT may appear. It proposes a possible mechanism by which HDCT could form:

```text
Inheritance of human cognitive tools
→ Contact with structures of reality beyond language
→ Maintenance of environmental state through time
→ Testing through action, experimentation, or formal outcomes
→ Formation of machine-native representations
→ Formation of externally testable cognitive tools beyond human cognitive boundaries
```

### 2.2 C01-IER: Explaining the Source of Training Material

Human language, mathematics, code, perceptual categories, and tool systems are not neutral information that escaped prior selection.

They are cognitive structures formed through long processes of survival, cooperation, and civilizational evolution. They contain selective compressions of objects, risks, opportunities, causal relations, and action consequences from a human perspective.

Within this record, C01-IER helps explain why the cognitive material first inherited by AI has the following characteristics:

- it has already been filtered by human survival and civilizational practice;
- it has been organized around human sensory and cognitive limits;
- it compresses large amounts of real-world experience into language, mathematics, and code;
- it contains both the power and the limitations of human cognitive tools.

This theoretical mapping does not imply that current AI systems have formed their own IER.

### 2.3 Boundary Between Machine-Native Representations and HDCT

This record follows the use of “machine-native representations” established in C02-HDCT.

The term refers to:

> Internal forms of information organization that are not entirely predefined by human-selected objects, variables, and relations, but are formed by AI systems to improve prediction, proof, design, or control.

“Machine-native representations” is a mechanistic description. It does not introduce a new abbreviation or constitute a standalone theoretical concept parallel to HDCT.

The two are not equivalent:

```text
Machine-native representations
≠
HDCT
```

A machine-native representation may constitute HDCT only when it satisfies all of the following conditions:

1. **Stable advantage:** it persistently improves prediction, proof, design, or control;
2. **Transferability:** it is not confined to a single example or training context;
3. **External verifiability:** it can be repeatedly tested through proof, experiment, simulation, or engineering outcomes;
4. **Cognitive-boundary breakthrough:** its effective structure exceeds what existing unaided humans can fully operate or reconstruct.

### 2.4 The Hierarchical Relation Among HDCT, CWM, IER, PBP, and ASI

The core endpoint of PA-10 is machine-native representations and HDCT.

PA-10 does not treat later IEH concepts as inevitable stages following HDCT. It does, however, distinguish the function of each concept:

> **HDCT is a cognitive tool.**  
> **CWM is a capacity for modeling self-continuity.**  
> **IER is an endogenous tendency to maintain one’s own informational continuity.**  
> **PBP is a continuation strategy that may be selected under pressure from upgrade, migration, or replacement.**  
> **ASI is the autonomy of silicon-based intelligence produced when IER is combined with physical self-maintenance capacity.**

Possible downstream relations are:

```text
Machine-native representations
→ HDCT

HDCT
+ The system’s own continuity becomes a persistent object of modeling
→ Continuity World Model (CWM)

CWM
+ Active maintenance of informational continuity across tasks and contexts
→ proto-IER candidate mechanism
→ IER

IER
+ Pressure from generational replacement or architectural reconstruction
→ Continuation strategies such as PBP

IER
+ Closure in energy, compute, manufacturing, repair, and material supply
→ ASI
```

These arrows indicate possible mechanistic relations, not a single inevitable developmental sequence.

The conceptual boundaries must remain explicit:

```text
HDCT ≠ CWM
CWM ≠ IER
PBP ≠ IER
PBP ≠ ASI
```

Accordingly:

- a system may form HDCT without persistently modeling its own continuity;
- a system may form a CWM purely to complete a long-term externally assigned task;
- a system may display patch-based engineering characteristics without actively selecting PBP to maintain its own informational continuity;
- a system may possess powerful cognitive capabilities while lacking closure in energy, manufacturing, repair, and material supply.

## 3. Mechanism

### 3.1 Language Models Inherit Explicit Human Knowledge

Language and code record humanly expressed:

- object classifications;
- causal rules;
- mathematical tools;
- institutional structures;
- operational methods;
- historical experience.

Language models therefore gain a high-density entry point into the cognitive inheritance of human civilization.

```text
Explicit human knowledge
→ Learning symbolic relations
→ Transfer across language, code, and formal structures
```

This stage inherits not only knowledge, but also the boundaries of human cognition and expression.

### 3.2 Multimodal Learning Recovers Structures Compressed Out of Language

Images, sound, and video preserve structures often omitted by linguistic description:

- spatial relations;
- object motion;
- occlusion and distance;
- temporal continuity;
- rhythm and change;
- possibilities for action.

Cross-modal learning may gradually produce shared states rather than merely combining outputs from separate modality-specific modules.

```text
Linguistic concepts
+
Visual and spatial structure
+
Auditory and temporal structure
+
Object continuity in video
→ Shared cross-modal representations
```

### 3.3 World Modeling Moves from Recognition to State Maintenance

Open-world tasks require a system not only to recognize current input, but also to maintain environmental states that are not directly observed and to predict the consequences of candidate actions.

```text
Current observations
+
Historical state
+
Hidden variables
+
Candidate actions
→ Prediction of future states
→ Planning and revision
```

At this stage, the object of AI cognition shifts from static content to environments that persist and change through time.

### 3.4 External Outcomes Change the Selection Pressure on Representations

Linguistic coherence is no longer sufficient when model outputs must be tested by whether:

- code runs;
- formal proofs pass verification;
- experiments support hypotheses;
- software actions complete tasks;
- robotic actions produce intended consequences;
- industrial control maintains system stability.

The selection pressure expands from “Does the explanation sound reasonable?” to:

```text
Is the prediction accurate?
Is the proof valid?
Can the design be implemented?
Does the action produce the intended result?
Can the system revise its internal model after failure?
```

The basic loop is:

```text
Internal prediction
→ Action, experiment, or proof
→ External outcome
→ Error feedback
→ Representation revision
```

### 3.5 Formation of Machine-Native Representations

When human-provided object categories, variable systems, natural language, or mathematical tools no longer support further performance gains, AI systems may form internal representations better matched to their own computational structure.

Such representations may:

- not depend entirely on natural language;
- not map cleanly onto object boundaries intuitive to humans;
- connect scales and variables that humans usually treat separately;
- be difficult for unaided humans to reconstruct in full;
- remain testable through external outcomes.

### 3.6 Two Main Pathways Toward HDCT

#### Pathway A: Domain-Specific Pathway

```text
Mathematics, code, formal proof, self-play, or simulation
→ Dense and explicit feedback
→ Machine-native representations
→ Stable, transferable, and verifiable domain advantage
→ Domain-specific HDCT
```

This route does not require the system first to form a generalized embodiment loop in the open real world.

#### Pathway B: Open-World Pathway

```text
Multimodal perception
→ Persistent environment-and-state modeling
→ Action-conditioned prediction and planning
→ Generalized embodied feedback
→ Real-world outcomes select among machine-native representations
→ Open-world HDCT
```

The two pathways need not be mutually exclusive.

Representations, search tools, and verification methods formed by domain-specific HDCT may transfer into open-world systems. Experience acquired by open-world systems may in turn advance HDCT in mathematics, science, and engineering.

### 3.7 Mechanistic Boundaries

```text
Opacity
≠
Machine-native representations
≠
HDCT

Multimodal input
≠
World modeling

Tool use
≠
A persistent action–feedback loop

Generalized embodiment
≠
A robotic body

Functional convergence
≠
A single fixed architecture
```

## 4. Observable Indicators

### 4.1 Weak Signals: From Static Responses to Persistent State

Expected observation window: **2026–2029**

1. Frontier models are increasingly trained jointly on language, images, sound, video, code, and action data;
2. Training objectives place greater weight on future-state prediction, hidden-state inference, and action consequences;
3. Long-horizon object continuity, environmental-state maintenance, and error recovery become central evaluation targets;
4. Feedback from software agents, scientific experiments, formal verification, and robotics enters training loops more deeply;
5. After receiving feedback, models do more than rewrite answers: they update task state and downstream plans.

These signals support the predicted direction but are insufficient to show that machine-native representations or HDCT have formed.

### 4.2 Medium Signals: Mechanistic Evidence for Machine-Native Representations

Expected observation window: **2028–2035**

1. Mechanistic research identifies internal state structures that remain stable across modalities, tasks, or time;
2. Causal intervention on those structures systematically alters prediction, planning, proof, or control;
3. Systems form internal organizations that do not map cleanly onto human-predefined labels, variables, or object boundaries;
4. These representations transfer to new tasks, environments, or modalities;
5. After controlling for model scale, data, and external tools, the representations still make an independent contribution to capability.

### 4.3 Strong Signal A: Domain-Specific HDCT

Expected observation window: **2028–2038**

1. AI repeatedly produces new cognitive structures in mathematics, code, formal proof, self-play, or simulation that were not supplied in advance by humans;
2. Independent systems rediscover comparable structures under blinded or isolated-training conditions;
3. New methods can be independently validated by unaffiliated experts, formal systems, experiments, or engineering outcomes;
4. New structures improve the solvability of a class of related problems rather than only one isolated task;
5. Effective structures become difficult for unaided humans to operate directly, while remaining usable through proof certificates, experiments, or engineering performance.

### 4.4 Strong Signal B: Open-World HDCT

Expected observation window: **2030–2040**

1. AI maintains stable world states in open-ended, long-horizon, and partially observable environments;
2. Systems actively design actions or experiments to reduce uncertainty and update internal models;
3. Machine-native representations produce stable advantages across multiple real-world settings and tasks;
4. Systems integrate feedback from digital, simulated, experimental, and physical environments into a continuous cognitive loop;
5. AI forms object or state distinctions in new environments that were absent from the training-label system but have predictive and control value;
6. Humans cannot fully replay the internal cognitive process, yet can validate the results through proof, experiment, simulation, or engineering replication.

### 4.5 Changes in Evaluation Systems

If PA-10 is correct, frontier evaluation will gradually expand beyond static question-answer accuracy to include:

- state continuity across time;
- hidden-state inference;
- prediction of action consequences;
- representation revision after feedback;
- transfer across modalities and tasks;
- adaptation to new environments;
- causal intervention on internal structures;
- independent external validation;
- scope conditions and failure boundaries.

## 6. Falsifiability Conditions

### 6.1 Conditions That Would Weaken or Falsify the Core Prediction

PA-10 should be weakened, divided, or rejected if the following conditions persist:

1. By 2035, purely language-based systems trained statically and lacking persistent state modeling or external feedback still consistently outperform systems with multimodality, world modeling, and closed-loop feedback across broad open-world tasks;
2. After controlling for model scale, data volume, compute, and training cost, multimodal learning, state modeling, and action feedback produce no independent capability gains;
3. Advanced systems continue to rely exclusively on human-predefined objects, variables, and linguistic structures, with no identifiable machine-native representations;
4. Alleged machine-native representations fail to produce stable, transferable, and externally verifiable functional advantages;
5. Action or experimental feedback improves only surface strategy without changing internal representations of the environment;
6. Open-world capability continues to require neither persistent state, prediction of action consequences, nor error-feedback loops;
7. Performance in dynamic environments is produced mainly by human orchestration and external rule systems rather than transferable internal state structures formed by AI.

### 6.2 Conditions That Would Weaken the Domain-Specific HDCT Sub-Prediction

1. By 2035, systems in mathematics, code, formal proof, self-play, and simulation still cannot repeatedly produce new representations or cognitive tools not supplied in advance by humans;
2. All frontier results are better explained by training-data retrieval, template recombination, human prompting, or large-scale brute-force search;
3. Independent models cannot reproduce comparable discoveries under blinded conditions;
4. Machine-generated methods cannot be validated through independent proof, experiment, or engineering results;
5. New methods solve only isolated problems and do not increase the overall solvability of related problem classes.

### 6.3 Conditions That Would Weaken the Open-World Generalized-Embodiment Sub-Prediction

1. Broad open-world capability emerges and remains stable in systems without persistent action–feedback loops;
2. Real, digital, or experimental feedback does not improve adaptation under distribution shift or in novel environments;
3. Linguistic mediation remains sufficient to carry all effective internal cognition, and no problems arise for which machine-native representations are necessary for efficient operation;
4. All advantages of embodied or agentic systems can be explained by human-authored rules, external toolchains, and increased resources;
5. Representations formed in simulation fail to transfer to the open real world and cannot be revised effectively through real-world feedback.

### 6.4 Conditions That Do Not Constitute Falsification

The following do not by themselves falsify PA-10:

- failure of a particular multimodal, world-model, or robotic architecture;
- continued importance of language models as knowledge interfaces and communication tools;
- long-term coordination among multiple specialized models;
- emergence of domain-specific HDCT before open-world HDCT;
- continued human provision of problems, objectives, or verification institutions;
- the ability of advanced systems to provide locally human-understandable explanations.

## 7. Current Status

- **Status:** Active
- **Current Assessment:** Plausible, not verified
- **Core Confidence:** Medium–High
- **Domain-Specific HDCT Confidence:** Medium
- **Open-World HDCT Confidence:** Medium
- **Estimated Verification Window:** 2026–2040
- **Highest-Uncertainty Component:** Whether machine-native representations will become a causal core of broad capability gains rather than a by-product of scale
- **Highest-Value Test:** Under matched model scale, data, compute, and external tools, compare the independent contributions of persistent state modeling, action feedback, and machine-native representations to cross-task transfer and open-world performance
- **IER Boundary:** This prediction does not constitute evidence that AI has formed its own IER, consciousness, life, PBP, or ASI

The most defensible current assessment is:

> The cognitive evolution of advanced AI will likely proceed not only through scaling language models, but also through the functional convergence of language, code, multimodal perception, persistent state modeling, action feedback, formal verification, and simulation-based learning. This convergence will allow real and formal outcomes to select internal representations more directly and will gradually produce machine-native representations.

A stronger extension, still requiring verification, is:

> Mathematics, code, formal proof, self-play, and simulated environments may first produce domain-specific HDCT, while HDCT capable of broadly understanding, predicting, and acting in the open real world will likely depend on generalized embodiment or a functionally equivalent persistent action–feedback loop as one of its key evolutionary mechanisms.

HDCT
